What is Data Science?

Data science is a discipline that combines maths, statistics and programming and, crucially, the ability to apply these skills to a business problem.

These cutting-edge techniques can unlock data that is too large, complex or real-time to be dealt with traditional analytics.

If we are able to take advantage of these new technologies and techniques in government, not only does it give us the potential to get better insights from our existing data, but we can also start using new sources of data which we couldnâ€™t previously work with such as social media, data scraped from the web, phone calls, images and web logs.

Who are we?

We are a joint team working across the Cabinet Office including the Government Digital Service, the Government Office for Science and the Office for National Statistics.

As a team, we raise awareness about the potential of data science and help to embed new approaches and skills in government.

What do we do?

Projects

We work closely with departments to help solve their policy challenges in addition to working on projects that support the centre.

Learn more

Capability

We are building capability by training existing talent, recruiting new talent and working with expert, external companies. We have also built several communities to encourage the exchange of knowledge and expertise.

Technology and data

We are working to improve accessibility to high quality technology and data. We work with the GDS technology team to help integrate data science solutions in to new IT rollouts.

Ethics and transparency

We recognise that data science may pose ethical challenges. In addition to working openly, we are building a robust ethical framework against which data science projects can be assessed.
